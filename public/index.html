Welcome to the World of Ping Pong

Ping Pong, officially known as table tennis, is one of the world's most popular and exciting sports. This fast-paced game combines lightning-quick reflexes, strategic thinking, and incredible hand-eye coordination. Whether you're a casual player enjoying a friendly match in your basement or a competitive athlete training for international tournaments, ping pong offers endless entertainment and physical benefits.

Professional players demonstrate the intensity and skill required in competitive table tennis

The history of ping pong dates back to the 1880s when it evolved from lawn tennis as an indoor alternative. Originally called "whiff-whaff," the game quickly gained popularity in England and spread throughout Europe and Asia. Today, table tennis is governed by the International Table Tennis Federation (ITTF) and is played in over 220 countries and territories.

Official Ping Pong Rules

üèì Serving

The ball must be thrown up at least 6 inches and hit on the way down. It must bounce once on your side, then once on opponent's side.

üéØ Scoring

Games are played to 11 points. You must win by 2 points. Players alternate serves every 2 points.

üîÑ Rally

After the serve, players alternate hitting the ball. The ball must cross the net and land on the opponent's side.

üèÜ Match

A match is typically best of 5 or 7 games. Professional matches can go up to 7 games.

Master the Techniques

üèì Forehand Drive

The most fundamental stroke in ping pong. Keep your elbow close to your body, rotate your waist, and follow through across your body.

üîÑ Backhand Drive

Essential for quick exchanges. Keep your wrist firm, use your elbow as a pivot, and contact the ball in front of your body.

‚ö° Power Serve

Start with the ball in your palm, throw it up 6+ inches, and strike with a firm wrist snap for maximum speed and spin.
